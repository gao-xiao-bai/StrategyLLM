Strategy:

1. **Read and understand the question**: Parse the question to identify the entities, relations, and conditions mentioned.
2. **Identify the key concepts**: Extract the main concepts and keywords from the question, such as entities, actions, and properties.
3. **Retrieve relevant information**: Search for relevant facts and information related to the key concepts, either from external knowledge or internal memory.
4. **Perform necessary calculations or conversions**: If required, perform calculations or conversions to obtain the necessary values or units.
5. **Make inferences and logical connections**: Use the retrieved information and perform logical deductions to connect the concepts and answer the question.
6. **Evaluate the answer**: Check if the inferred answer satisfies the conditions mentioned in the question.
7. **Provide a yes or no answer**: Based on the evaluation, provide a simple "yes" or "no" answer to the question.

This strategy is general, concise, and breaks down the task into easy-to-solve subtasks. It does not include specific information from the examples and can be applied to a wide range of questions that require multi-hop reasoning.
