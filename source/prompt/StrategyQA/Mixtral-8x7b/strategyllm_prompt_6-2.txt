Strategy:
1. Understand the question: Determine the intent of the question, whether it is a factual question, a yes/no question, or a comparison question.
2. Identify key concepts: Recognize the main concepts in the question that will be necessary for finding the answer.
3. Gather background knowledge: Retrieve any general knowledge required to answer the question, including contextual information about the key concepts.
4. Search for information: Consult one or more databases, knowledge graphs, or other information sources to find specific facts related to the question.
5. Evaluate the information: Assess the credibility and relevance of the information gathered, and filter out any sources that are not reliable.
6. Synthesize the answer: Combine the relevant information to form a coherent and accurate answer to the question.
7. Verify the answer: Cross-check the answer against other sources or use reasoning to ensure its validity.
8. Present the answer: Formulate the answer in the required format, whether it is a simple yes/no or a more elaborate explanation.

Examples:
