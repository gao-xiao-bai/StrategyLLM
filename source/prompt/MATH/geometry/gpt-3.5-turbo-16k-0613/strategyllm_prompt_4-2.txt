   - Area of each sector = $\frac{1}{6} \cdot \pi \cdot 144$
   - Area of each sector = $24\pi$

   - Area of the triangle = $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 12 \cdot 12 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$
   - Area of the triangle = $72\sqrt{3}$
   - Area of the shaded region = $(2 \cdot 24\pi) - 72\sqrt{3}$
   - Area of the shaded region = $48\pi - 72\sqrt{3}$

$y = x/2$
$(-a, -a), (a, -a), (-a, a), (a, a)$

$a/2 = -a$
Solving for $a$, we find $a = 0$.

Therefore, the line intersects the square at the points $(0, 0)$.

Since the line passes through the origin $(0, 0)$, it divides the square into two congruent triangles. The side length of the square is $2a$, so the side length of each congruent triangle is $a$.

c. Calculate the perimeter of one congruent quadrilateral:
Each congruent quadrilateral consists of two congruent triangles and two congruent rectangles. The perimeter of one congruent quadrilateral is equal to the sum of the perimeters of the two triangles and the two rectangles.

Perimeter of one triangle = sum of the lengths of its three sides
Perimeter of one rectangle = sum of the lengths of its four sides

Triangle:
Side 1: $a$
Side 2: $a/2$
Side 3: Hypotenuse of the right triangle formed by the line and the side of the square

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can find the length of the hypotenuse:

$a^2/4 + a^2 = c^2$
$5a^2/4 = c^2$
$c = \sqrt{5}a/2$

Rectangle:

Question: A right circular cylinder with radius 2 is inscribed in a hemisphere with radius 5 so that its bases are parallel to the base of the hemisphere. What is the height of this cylinder?

Solution:
1. Given information:
   - Radius of the cylinder (r) = 2
   - Radius of the hemisphere (R) = 5

2. Subtask: Find the height of the cylinder.

3. Subtask: Find the radius of the cylinder's base.

   - The radius of the cylinder's base is equal to the radius of the hemisphere's base, which is R.

   - Therefore, the radius of the cylinder's base (r_base) = 5.

4. Subtask: Find the height of the cylinder using the Pythagorean theorem.

   - The height of the cylinder (h) can be found using the Pythagorean theorem, where h^2 = R^2 - r_base^2.

   - Substituting the values, h^2 = 5^2 - 2^2 = 25 - 4 = 21.

   - Taking the square root of both sides, h = sqrt(21).

5. Verify the solution:

   - The height of the cylinder is sqrt(21).

6. Final solution:

   The height of the cylinder is sqrt(21).

Answer: The answer is sqrt(21).
