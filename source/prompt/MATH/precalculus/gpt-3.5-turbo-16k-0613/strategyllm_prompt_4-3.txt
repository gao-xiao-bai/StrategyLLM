Question: Convert the point $\left( 8, \frac{7 \pi}{6} \right)$ in polar coordinates to rectangular coordinates.

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This problem involves converting polar coordinates to rectangular coordinates.

2. Review the relevant concepts and formulas:
   - The conversion formulas are:
     - $x = r \cos(\theta)$
     - $y = r \sin(\theta)$

3. Analyze the given information:
   - The given point is $\left( 8, \frac{7 \pi}{6} \right)$ in polar coordinates.

4. Solve the problem step by step:
   - Using the conversion formulas:
     - $x = 8 \cos\left(\frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)$
     - $y = 8 \sin\left(\frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)$
   - Evaluating the trigonometric functions:
     - $x = 8 \cdot \left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = -4\sqrt{3}$
     - $y = 8 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = -4$
   
5. Check and verify the solution:
   - The obtained rectangular coordinates are $(-4\sqrt{3}, -4)$.
   - To verify, we can substitute these values back into the conversion formulas:
     - $r = \sqrt{(-4\sqrt{3})^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{48 + 16} = \sqrt{64} = 8$
     - $\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{-4}{-4\sqrt{3}}\right) = \arctan\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right) = \frac{\pi}{6}$
   - The polar coordinates obtained are $\left( 8, \frac{\pi}{6} \right)$.
   - The obtained polar coordinates match the given polar coordinates, so the solution is correct.

6. Communicate the final answer:
   - The rectangular coordinates of the point $\left( 8, \frac{7 \pi}{6} \right)$ are $(-4\sqrt{3}, -4)$.

Answer: The answer is $(-4\sqrt{3}, -4)$.

2. Review the relevant concepts and formulas:

3. Analyze the given information:
   - We are given two matrices: $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 5 & -3 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} 8 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$.

4. Solve the problem step by step:
   - To compute the product of the two matrices, we multiply the corresponding elements in each row of the first matrix with the corresponding elements in each column of the second matrix, and then sum the products.
   - Let's calculate the elements of the resulting matrix:
     - The first element of the resulting matrix is $2 \cdot 8 + 0 \cdot 1 = 16$.
     - The second element of the resulting matrix is $2 \cdot (-2) + 0 \cdot 1 = -4$.
     - The third element of the resulting matrix is $5 \cdot 8 + (-3) \cdot 1 = 37$.
     - The fourth element of the resulting matrix is $5 \cdot (-2) + (-3) \cdot 1 = -13$.

5. Check and verify the solution:
   - The calculated elements of the resulting matrix are: $\begin{pmatrix} 16 & -4 \\ 37 & -13 \end{pmatrix}$.
   - Comparing this with the given answer, we can see that the solution is correct.

6. Communicate the final answer:

Question: A line is parameterized by a parameter $t,$ so that the vector on the line at $t = 2$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix},$ and the vector on the line at $t = 3$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}.$  Find the vector on the line at $t = -7.$

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This problem involves vectors.

2. Review the relevant concepts and formulas:
   - The formula for finding a vector on a line given two points is $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v}_0 + t \mathbf{d},$ where $\mathbf{v}_0$ is a vector on the line and $\mathbf{d}$ is the direction vector of the line.

3. Analyze the given information:
   - We are given two vectors on the line at $t = 2$ and $t = 3.$
   - We need to find the vector on the line at $t = -7.$

4. Solve the problem step by step:
   - Let $\mathbf{v}_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ be the vector on the line at $t = 2.$
   - Let $\mathbf{d} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}$ be the direction vector of the line.
   - Substitute $t = -7$ into the formula $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v}_0 + t \mathbf{d}$ to find the vector on the line at $t = -7.$
     - $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} + (-7) \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}$
     - $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -14 \\ 56 \end{pmatrix}$
     - $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -13 \\ 60 \end{pmatrix}$

5. Check and verify the solution:
   - The obtained vector $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -13 \\ 60 \end{pmatrix}$ satisfies the given conditions.
   - Substituting $t = -7$ into the formula $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v}_0 + t \mathbf{d},$ we get $\begin{pmatrix} -13 \\ 60 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} + (-7) \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix},$ which is true.

Answer: The answer is $\begin{pmatrix} -13 \\ 60 \end{pmatrix}.$
