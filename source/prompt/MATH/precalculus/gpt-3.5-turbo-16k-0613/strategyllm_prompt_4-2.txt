Strategy:
1. Identify the type of problem:
    - Determine if it is a problem involving functions, coordinates, matrices, or vectors.
    - This will help determine the appropriate subtasks to solve the problem.

2. For problems involving functions:
    a. Determine the operation being performed (e.g., rotation, composition, finding inverse) and identify the function form.
    b. Identify the unknown variable or constant in the function.
    c. Solve for the unknown variable or constant using algebraic manipulation.

3. For problems involving coordinates:
    a. Determine if the given coordinates are in polar or rectangular form.
    b. Convert the given coordinates to the desired form using the appropriate formulas.
    c. Substitute the given values into the formulas to calculate the converted coordinates.

4. For problems involving matrices:
    a. Identify the operation being performed (e.g., matrix multiplication, matrix addition) and write out the given matrices.
    b. Apply the corresponding rules for the specified operation.
    c. Perform the necessary calculations to obtain the resulting matrix.

5. For problems involving vectors and lines:
    a. Determine the parameterization of the line using the given vectors at specific values of t.
    b. Write out the equation of the line in vector form using the parameterization.
    c. Substitute the given value of t into the equation to obtain the vector on the line at the desired value of t.

6. Repeat steps 1-5 for each problem in the task.

7. Verify the solutions using any available tools or methods (e.g., graphing functions, checking calculations).

8. Double-check the solutions to ensure accuracy and correct any errors if necessary.

9. Write the final answers in the appropriate format (e.g., function form, coordinate form, matrix form, vector form).

10. Check that all problems in the precalculus subject have been solved.

Question: The function
\[f(z) = \frac{(-1 + i \sqrt{3}) z + (-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i)}{2}\]represents a rotation around some complex number $c$. Find $c$.

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This is a problem involving functions and complex numbers.

2. For problems involving functions:
   a. The operation being performed is rotation.
   b. The function form is given as $f(z) = \frac{(-1 + i \sqrt{3}) z + (-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i)}{2}$.
   c. The unknown variable or constant in the function is $c$.

3. Solve for $c$ using algebraic manipulation:
   a. Let $f(z) = w$, where $w$ is a complex number.
   b. Substitute $w$ into the function form: $w = \frac{(-1 + i \sqrt{3}) z + (-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i)}{2}$.
   c. Multiply both sides of the equation by 2 to eliminate the fraction: $2w = (-1 + i \sqrt{3}) z + (-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i)$.
   d. Rearrange the equation to isolate $z$: $2w + (1 - i \sqrt{3}) z = -2 \sqrt{3} - 18i$.
   e. Substitute $z = c$ into the equation: $2w + (1 - i \sqrt{3}) c = -2 \sqrt{3} - 18i$.
   f. Solve for $c$ by isolating it on one side of the equation: $(1 - i \sqrt{3}) c = -2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w$.
   g. Divide both sides of the equation by $(1 - i \sqrt{3})$: $c = \frac{-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w}{1 - i \sqrt{3}}$.
   h. Simplify the expression: $c = \frac{-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w}{1 - i \sqrt{3}} \cdot \frac{1 + i \sqrt{3}}{1 + i \sqrt{3}}$.
   i. Multiply the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of $1 - i \sqrt{3}$: $c = \frac{(-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w)(1 + i \sqrt{3})}{1 + 3}$.
   j. Simplify the expression: $c = \frac{(-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w)(1 + i \sqrt{3})}{4}$.
   k. Expand the numerator: $c = \frac{-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w + i \sqrt{3}(-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w)}{4}$.
   l. Simplify the expression: $c = \frac{-2 \sqrt{3} - 18i - 2w - 6 - 6i \sqrt{3} - 6i - 2w \sqrt{3} - 18i \sqrt{3} - 2w \sqrt{3}}{4}$.
   m. Combine like terms: $c = \frac{-4w - 2w \sqrt{3} - 2 \sqrt{3} - 6i - 6i \sqrt{3} - 18i - 18i \sqrt{3}}{4}$.
   n. Simplify the expression: $c = \frac{-4w - 2w \sqrt{3} - 2 \sqrt{3} - 6i - 6i \sqrt{3} - 18i - 18i \sqrt{3}}{4}$.
   o. Divide each term by 4: $c = \frac{-w - \frac{w \sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{3i}{2} - \frac{3i \sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{9i}{2} - \frac{9i \sqrt{3}}{2}}{1}$.
   p. Simplify the expression: $c = -\frac{w}{4} - \frac{w \sqrt{3}}{8} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{8} - \frac{3i}{8} - \frac{3i \sqrt{3}}{8} - \frac{9i}{8} - \frac{9i \sqrt{3}}{8}$.

Answer: The answer is $\sqrt{3}-5i$.



Question: Convert the point $\left( 8, \frac{7 \pi}{6} \right)$ in polar coordinates to rectangular coordinates.
Answer: The answer is (-4\sqrt{3},-4)

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This problem involves converting coordinates from polar form to rectangular form.

2. For problems involving coordinates:
   a. The given coordinates are in polar form.
   b. To convert from polar to rectangular coordinates, we use the formulas:
      - $x = r \cos(\theta)$
      - $y = r \sin(\theta)$
   c. Substitute the given values into the formulas to calculate the converted coordinates.

3. Applying the formulas:
   - Given: $r = 8$, $\theta = \frac{7 \pi}{6}$
   - $x = 8 \cos\left(\frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)$
   - $y = 8 \sin\left(\frac{7 \pi}{6}\right)$

4. Calculating the values:
   - $x = 8 \cdot \left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) = -4\sqrt{3}$
   - $y = 8 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = -4$

Answer: The answer is (-4\sqrt{3},-4)



Question: Compute $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 5 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 8 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}.$

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This is a problem involving matrices.

2. For problems involving matrices:
   a. The operation being performed is matrix multiplication.
   b. The given matrices are $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 5 & -3 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} 8 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
   c. Apply the rules of matrix multiplication to obtain the resulting matrix.

   To multiply two matrices, we need to take the dot product of each row of the first matrix with each column of the second matrix.

   Let's calculate the resulting matrix step by step:

   - For the first element of the resulting matrix:
     - Multiply the first row of the first matrix with the first column of the second matrix:
       $2 \cdot 8 + 0 \cdot 1 = 16$
     - So, the first element of the resulting matrix is 16.

   - For the second element of the resulting matrix:
     - Multiply the first row of the first matrix with the second column of the second matrix:
       $2 \cdot (-2) + 0 \cdot 1 = -4$
     - So, the second element of the resulting matrix is -4.

   - For the third element of the resulting matrix:
     - Multiply the second row of the first matrix with the first column of the second matrix:
       $5 \cdot 8 + (-3) \cdot 1 = 37$
     - So, the third element of the resulting matrix is 37.

   - For the fourth element of the resulting matrix:
     - Multiply the second row of the first matrix with the second column of the second matrix:
       $5 \cdot (-2) + (-3) \cdot 1 = -13$
     - So, the fourth element of the resulting matrix is -13.

3. The resulting matrix is $\begin{pmatrix} 16 & -4 \\ 37 & -13 \end{pmatrix}$.

Answer: The answer is $\begin{pmatrix} 16 & -4 \\ 37 & -13 \end{pmatrix}$.



Question: A line is parameterized by a parameter $t,$ so that the vector on the line at $t = 2$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix},$ and the vector on the line at $t = 3$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}.$  Find the vector on the line at $t = -7.$

Solution:
1. Identify the type of problem:
   - This problem involves vectors and lines.

2. For problems involving vectors and lines:
   a. The line is parameterized by a parameter $t,$ so we can write the equation of the line in vector form as $\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{a} + t \mathbf{d},$ where $\mathbf{v}(t)$ is the vector on the line at $t,$ $\mathbf{a}$ is a known vector on the line, and $\mathbf{d}$ is the direction vector of the line.
   b. We are given that $\mathbf{v}(2) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{v}(3) = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}.$
   c. Substitute the given values of $t$ and the corresponding vectors into the equation to obtain a system of equations.
      - For $t = 2,$ we have $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + 2 \mathbf{d}.$
      - For $t = 3,$ we have $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + 3 \mathbf{d}.$
   d. Solve the system of equations to find the values of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{d}.$
      - Subtracting the first equation from the second equation, we get $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + 3 \mathbf{d} - (\mathbf{a} + 2 \mathbf{d}).$
      - Simplifying, we have $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + 3 \mathbf{d} - \mathbf{a} - 2 \mathbf{d}.$
      - Combining like terms, we get $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{d}.$
      - Substituting this value of $\mathbf{d}$ into the first equation, we have $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + 2 \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}.$
      - Simplifying, we get $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a} + \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -16 \end{pmatrix}.$
      - Subtracting $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -16 \end{pmatrix}$ from both sides, we have $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -16 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a}.$
      - Simplifying, we get $\begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 20 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{a}.$

3. Substitute the value of $t = -7$ into the equation $\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{a} + t \mathbf{d}$ to find the vector on the line at $t = -7.$
   - We have $\mathbf{v}(-7) = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 20 \end{pmatrix} + (-7) \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}.$
   - Simplifying, we get $\mathbf{v}(-7) = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 20 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -14 \\ 56 \end{pmatrix}.$
   - Adding the vectors, we have $\mathbf{v}(-7) = \begin{pmatrix} -3 + (-14) \\ 20 + 56 \end{pmatrix}.$
   - Simplifying, we get $\mathbf{v}(-7) = \begin{pmatrix} -17 \\ 76 \end{pmatrix}.$

Answer: The answer is $\begin{pmatrix} -17 \\ 76 \end{pmatrix}.$
