Strategy:
Here is a strategy to solve the problems in counting and probability:

**1. Understand the problem**: Read the problem carefully and identify the key elements, such as the numbers, events, and rules involved.

**2. Identify the type of problem**: Classify the problem as either a counting problem (e.g., permutation, combination, counting objects) or a probability problem (e.g., conditional probability, independent events, Bayes' theorem).

**3. Break down the problem into smaller parts**: If the problem involves multiple steps or events, break it down into smaller, manageable parts. This can help to identify patterns and relationships between the different parts.

**4. Use counting principles**: If the problem involves counting, use counting principles such as the fundamental counting principle, permutations, and combinations to solve the problem.

**5. Use probability rules**: If the problem involves probability, use probability rules such as the addition rule, multiplication rule, and Bayes' theorem to solve the problem.

**6. Check for symmetry and pattern recognition**: Many counting and probability problems exhibit symmetry or patterns. Check for these patterns and use them to simplify the solution or identify the answer.

**7. Use fleshing-out techniques**: If the problem involves sequences or arrangements, use techniques like "fleshing out" to enumerate all possible outcomes and then count or calculate the desired probability.

**8. Evaluate and simplify the answer**: After solving the problem, evaluate the answer to ensure it makes sense in the context of the problem. Simplify the answer whenever possible to make it more intuitive and easier to understand.

By following these subtasks, you should be able to develop a strategy to solve a wide range of counting and probability problems.
