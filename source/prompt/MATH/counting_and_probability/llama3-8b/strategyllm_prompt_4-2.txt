**Step 3: Break Down the Problem**

* Find the total number of ways to arrange the digits 2, 3, 5, and 7 to form a four-digit number.
* Find the number of ways to arrange the digits to form an odd number.
* Calculate the probability of the event (the number being odd) by dividing the number of ways to form an odd number by the total number of ways to form a four-digit number.

**Step 4: Apply the Correct Formula or Technique**
We can use the formula for the number of permutations of n items taken r at a time, which is:

P(n, r) = n! / (n-r)!

where n is the total number of items, r is the number of items taken, and ! denotes the factorial function.

In this case, we have n = 4 (the number of digits) and r = 4 (the number of digits in the four-digit number). Therefore, the total number of ways to arrange the digits is:

P(4, 4) = 4! / (4-4)! = 4! = 24

* The first digit is 3, 5, or 7 (3 possibilities)
* The remaining three digits can be arranged in 3! = 6 ways

Therefore, the number of ways to arrange the digits to form an odd number is:

3 × 6 = 18

**Step 5: Simplify the Calculation**
We can simplify the calculation by dividing the number of ways to form an odd number by the total number of ways to form a four-digit number:

P(odd) = 18 / 24 = 3/4

**Step 6: Check the Answer**
We can check the answer by plugging it back into the original equation or verifying it with a simpler equivalent expression. In this case, the answer is correct.

**Answer:** The answer is 3/4.
