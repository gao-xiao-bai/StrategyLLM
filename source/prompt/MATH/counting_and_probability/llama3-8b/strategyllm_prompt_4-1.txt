2. **Identify the type of problem**: This is a counting problem, specifically a permutation problem.

3. **Determine the sample space**: The sample space is the set of all possible ways to arrange the 8 equilateral triangles on the octahedron.

4. **Count or calculate the desired probability**: We can use the permutation formula to calculate the number of ways to arrange the 8 equilateral triangles. Since the triangles are distinguishable, we can treat them as distinct objects. The formula for permutations is:

P(n, k) = n! / (n-k)!

where n is the total number of objects (8 triangles) and k is the number of objects being arranged (8 triangles).

P(8, 8) = 8! / (8-8)! = 8! / 1! = 8 × 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 40,320

However, this includes arrangements where the triangles are rotated to look the same. To account for this, we need to divide the result by the number of ways to rotate each triangle, which is 6 (since there are 6 possible rotations of an equilateral triangle).

40,320 / 6 = 6,720

5. **Simplify the result**: The result is 6,720.

6. **Verify the answer**: We can verify the answer by checking that it satisfies the conditions given in the problem. The answer is a positive integer, which makes sense since it represents the number of distinguishable ways to construct the octahedron.
