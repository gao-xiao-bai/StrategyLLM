Strategy:
Here is a general strategy to solve counting and probability problems:

1. **Read and understand the problem**: Read the problem carefully and understand what is being asked. Identify the key elements, such as the number of objects, events, or choices.

2. **Identify the type of problem**: Determine whether the problem involves counting, probability, or a combination of both. Identify the specific concept or technique required to solve the problem, such as permutations, combinations, or conditional probability.

3. **Determine the sample space**: Identify the total number of possible outcomes or choices. This can involve listing all possible outcomes, creating a diagram, or using a formula to calculate the total number of possibilities.

4. **Apply counting techniques**: Use techniques such as permutations, combinations, or the fundamental counting principle to calculate the number of ways to achieve a specific outcome or set of outcomes.

5. **Apply probability concepts**: If the problem involves probability, calculate the probability of a specific outcome or set of outcomes by dividing the number of favorable outcomes by the total number of possible outcomes.

6. **Simplify and evaluate expressions**: Simplify any expressions or formulas obtained in the previous steps, and evaluate them to obtain the final answer.

7. **Check and verify the answer**: Check the answer to ensure it makes sense in the context of the problem, and verify it by re-evaluating the problem or using a different approach.

By following this strategy, you should be able to solve a wide range of counting and probability problems, including the examples provided.

**Question:** The digits 2, 3, 5 and 7 are arranged randomly to form a four-digit number. What is the probability that the number is odd? Express your answer as a common fraction.

**Solution:**

**Step 1: Read and understand the problem**
The problem asks for the probability that a four-digit number formed by arranging the digits 2, 3, 5, and 7 is odd.

**Step 2: Identify the type of problem**
This problem involves probability, specifically the probability of an event (the number being odd) occurring.

**Step 3: Determine the sample space**
The sample space consists of all possible four-digit numbers that can be formed by arranging the digits 2, 3, 5, and 7. Since the digits are distinct, we can use the fundamental counting principle to calculate the total number of possible outcomes:

4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

So, there are 24 possible four-digit numbers.

**Step 4: Apply counting techniques**
To find the number of ways to form an odd number, we need to consider the last digit (units place) of the number. Since the number must be odd, the last digit must be either 3, 5, or 7. For each of these three possibilities, we can arrange the remaining three digits in 3! = 6 ways. Therefore, the number of ways to form an odd number is:

3 × 6 = 18

**Step 5: Apply probability concepts**
The probability of forming an odd number is the number of favorable outcomes (18) divided by the total number of possible outcomes (24):

P(odd) = 18/24

**Step 6: Simplify and evaluate expressions**
We can simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by 6:

P(odd) = 3/4

**Step 7: Check and verify the answer**
The answer makes sense in the context of the problem, and we can verify it by re-evaluating the problem or using a different approach.

**Answer:** The answer is 3/4
